# Sumo Terminology Glossary

## The People

- **Rikishi** – A sumo wrestler.
- **Yokozuna** – Grand Champion (highest rank).
- **Ōzeki** – Champion rank below Yokozuna.
- **Gyōji** – Referee in the ring.
- **Shimpan** – Ringside judge.
- **Tate-gyōji** – Highest-ranking referee.

## The Ring & Arena

- **Dohyō** – The raised clay ring where matches take place.
- **Kokugikan** – The famous national sumo arena in Tokyo.
- **Tawara** – The straw bales forming the circular boundary.
- **Tokudawara** – The four protruding straw sections at north, south, east, and west.
- **Janome** – The sand surrounding the ring, used to show footprints and whether someone stepped out.

## The Lines & Starting Position

- **Shikiri-sen** – The two white lines in the centre where wrestlers crouch before charging.
- **Shikiri** – The ritualized stare-down and preparation before the bout.
- **Tachiai** – The explosive clash when the bout begins.

## Clothing

- **Mawashi** – The thick silk or cotton belt/loincloth worn by wrestlers.
- **Sagari** – The hanging cords on the front of the mawashi.
- **Kesho-mawashi** – Ornate apron worn during introductions.
- **Yokozuna-tsuna** – The giant rope worn by a Yokozuna during ceremonies.

## Match Terms

- **Torikumi** – An individual match pairing.
- **Honbasho** – A professional tournament.
- **Makuuchi** – Highest professional division.
- **Kinboshi** – When a lower-ranked wrestler beats a Yokozuna.
- **Fusen-shō** – Win by default when an opponent withdraws.
- **Mono-ii** – Review by the ringside judges.

## Winning & Losing

- **Kimarite** – Official winning move.
- **Yorikiri** – Force out; the most common winning technique.
- **Oshidashi** – Push opponent out without grabbing.
- **Uwatenage** – Throw using an outside belt grip.
- **Hatakikomi** – Pull/slap opponent to the ground.

## Rituals

- **Shio** – Salt thrown into the ring.
- **Chikara-mizu** – Ritual water drunk before a bout.
- **Chikara-gami** – Paper used after drinking the water.
- **Shiko** – The famous leg-lift stomp.
- **Dohyō-iri** – Ceremonial ring-entering ceremony.

## Common Terminology Mistakes

Many Western fans incorrectly refer to the entire ring as the "dohyō" and the straw boundary as "sandbags".

### Correct Terminology

- The raised clay platform is the **Dohyō**.
- The straw bales forming the circular boundary are the **Tawara**.
- The surrounding sand is the **Janome**.
- The white starting lines are the **Shikiri-sen**.
- The wrestlers are **Rikishi** wearing **Mawashi**.

## Typical Match Flow

1. **Dohyō-iri** (ring-entering ceremony)
2. **Shio** (salt purification)
3. **Shikiri** (pre-bout preparation and stare-down)
4. Wrestlers crouch at the **Shikiri-sen**
5. **Tachiai** (initial charge)
6. Match proceeds
7. Winner determined by a **Kimarite**
8. **Gyōji** signals the winner
9. **Shimpan** may call a **Mono-ii** if the result is disputed

## Sumo Ring Components

- **Dohyō** – Raised clay platform
- **Tawara** – Straw boundary bales
- **Tokudawara** – Four outward-facing special bales
- **Janome** – Sand surrounding the ring
- **Shikiri-sen** – Starting lines
